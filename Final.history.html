The History of Bonsai

The origin of Bonsai traces its roots to early China, thousands of years ago even though the word "Bonsai" is of Japanese origin. The Chinese used a method entitled "pun-sai" to grow dwarf trees within the confinement of smaller pots. Bonsai was considered a hobby of only the noble within the early days of China, but the Japanese began to incorporate Bonsai with their society as a way to promote and influence forms of Zen Buddhism.

The practice of Bonsai is still fairly new to Western culture and society and it it is not as prominently found. English observation of dwarven trees were first recognized and recorded in 1637. It would not be until 1940, when Europe would launch its first English Bonsai book. The 19th Century saw the rise of Bonsai in The United States, as popular films such as The Karate Kid spurred an interest for American Society to investigate further the art of Bonsai.
